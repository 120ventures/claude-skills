# Idea Cradle - 120 Ventures

## Purpose

Generate a complete **Idea Cradle Eintrag** for 120 Ventures. When the user shares a venture idea, research everything needed and produce a filled-out Idea Cradle document.

## Workflow

### 1. Capture the idea

Listen to whatever the user shares — it can be a one-liner, a rambling voice-note transcript, or a detailed brief. Extract the core concept before researching.

### 2. Research (use Firecrawl / web search)

Before filling out the template, research the following:

- **Market landscape**: key players, incumbents, adjacent solutions
- **Competitor analysis**: direct and indirect competitors, their value propositions, pricing, weaknesses
- **Market size**: find data points for a rough top-down AND bottom-up sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM style, but keep it practical — no fake precision)
- **Trends**: macro trends supporting this idea (regulatory, demographic, technological, behavioral)
- **Investment signals**: recent funding rounds, acquisitions, partnerships in the space (last 6-12 months)
- **Customer pain points**: validate that the problems are real — look for forum posts, reviews, articles, complaints
- **Revenue models**: how do comparable businesses monetize?

Focus on the **Austrian/DACH market** first, then broaden to EU/global if relevant.

### 3. Fill out the Idea Cradle template

Use the research to produce a complete, well-reasoned Idea Cradle entry. Every field must be filled — no placeholders or "TBD".

### 4. Output format

Output the completed Idea Cradle as a clean markdown document using the exact template structure below. Write in **English** (120 Ventures working language for Idea Cradles).

---

## Template

```markdown
### Why should we do this?

Our [Products and Services]

help(s) [Customer Segment]

who wants to [jobs to be done]

by [verb e.g. reducing, avoiding] [customer pain]

and [verb e.g. increasing, enabling] [customer gain]

unlike [competing value proposition]

We're building [product or service] to solve [core problem], creating a future where [describe the change or outcome you want to see], and doing so through a sustainable, scalable model based on [revenue logic].

### Elevator Pitch:

- [One-liner — punchy, memorable, max 2 sentences]

**The problem**

- Problem #1
- Problem #2
- Problem #3

**The solution we aim at**

- Key benefit #1
- Key benefit #2
- Key benefit #3

### Target Market:

[Who exactly are we selling to? Be specific: demographics, psychographics, geography, company size if B2B. Quantify where possible.]

### Value Proposition:

[What is our unique value proposition? Why would customers choose us over the status quo or competitors?]

### Competitive Advantage:

[Describe how we can build long-term competitive advantages — network effects, data moats, switching costs, brand, regulatory positioning, etc.]

### Revenue Model:

[How are we going to make money? What are our revenue streams? Pricing logic, unit economics intuition.]

### Market size and growth potential:

- [Top-down estimate: total addressable market with source/logic]
- [Bottom-up estimate: realistic serviceable market based on customer count x ARPU]
- [Key market trends and how they may evolve in the next 2-5 years]

### Investment signals:

- [Where is the money going? Overview of investment activity in this space.]
- [Recent big investments: 2-4 notable funding rounds, acquisitions, or product launches]
- [Key trends in the last 6 months: 2-3 signals showing where investment is growing — new startups, increased funding, partnerships]
```

## Quality checklist

Before delivering, verify:

- [ ] Every section is filled with specific, researched content — no generic filler
- [ ] Market size includes both top-down and bottom-up perspectives
- [ ] At least 2-3 real competitors are named with their value propositions
- [ ] Investment signals reference real, recent events (with approximate dates)
- [ ] The "Why should we do this?" narrative flows logically from top to bottom
- [ ] Revenue model is concrete, not hand-wavy
- [ ] The elevator pitch is genuinely concise and compelling
- [ ] DACH/Austrian market context is addressed where relevant

## Tone

Write like a sharp venture builder presenting to partners — confident, concise, evidence-backed. No fluff, no hype. Show you've done the homework.
